Teele wali Masjid

The Teele Wali Masjid, a magnificent Sunni mosque nestled along the serene banks of the River Gomti in Lucknow, stands as an architectural marvel and a symbol of religious significance. Built during the Mughal era, between 1658 and 1660 under the supervision of Fidah Khan Koka, this grand mosque reflects the essence of Mughal architecture with its impressive domes and intricate design. Its historical importance is underscored by being one of the first monuments commissioned by Emperor Aurangzeb after ascending to the throne. The mosque, also known as Jama Masjid Shahi Teela Shah Peer Muhammad Sahab Teele Wali Masjid, holds not just religious significance but also attracts pilgrims and tourists alike, gathering in large numbers during auspicious occasions like Jum-Atul-Vida, Eid-Ul-Fitr, and Eid-Ul-Adha. The adjoining shrine of Shah Peer Muhammad adds to its reverence, making the Teele Wali Masjid not just a monument but a revered spiritual hub in the heart of Lucknow.

Do you know what is the heart of Lucknow.

The heart of Lucknow beats within its bustling streets, vibrant markets, and architectural wonders. Encapsulated by the timeless charm of the city, this core embodies the essence of its rich history and cultural heritage. From the majestic Bara Imambara to the graceful Rumi Gate, the heart of Lucknow pulsates with architectural marvels narrating tales of regal grandeur. The winding lanes of Hazratganj, the aromatic flavors of Awadhi cuisine, and the rhythmic echoes of Kathak dance performances in its theaters encapsulate the city's soul. It's where traditions intertwine seamlessly with modernity, where the past converges with the present, creating a symphony of sights, sounds, and flavors that resonate through the bustling heart of Lucknow.
